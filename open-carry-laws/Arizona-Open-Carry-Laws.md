### Key Changes in Arizona‚Äôs Gun Laws (2013‚Äì2023)

### 2013 ‚Äî Background Checks and Private Sales

Arizona enacts legislation to require background checks for firearms sold at gun shows, closing a loophole that previously exempted private sales from checks.

### 2014 ‚Äî Restoration of Rights for Non-Violent Offenders

The state introduces measures to restore firearm rights to individuals convicted of non-violent felony offenses, enhancing opportunities for rehabilitation.

### 2015 ‚Äî State Preemption of Local Firearm Regulations

Arizona strengthens state preemption laws, ensuring that local jurisdictions cannot enact firearm regulations that exceed state law.

### 2016 ‚Äî Concealed Carry Permits for Out-of-State Residents

Non-residents with valid concealed carry permits from other states gain the ability to carry concealed firearms in Arizona.

### 2017 ‚Äî ‚ÄúConstitutional Carry‚Äù for Concealed Carry

Arizona adopts ‚ÄúConstitutional Carry,‚Äù allowing eligible individuals to carry concealed firearms without a permit.

### 2018 ‚Äî Enhanced Background Check Reporting

Legislation is passed to improve reporting of mental health records to the National Instant Criminal Background Check System (NICS), bolstering efforts to prevent access to firearms by prohibited individuals.

### 2019 ‚Äî ‚ÄúRed Flag‚Äù Law Debate

Discussion arises around ‚Äúred flag‚Äù laws, which would allow law enforcement and family members to request firearm removal orders for individuals deemed dangerous.

### 2020 ‚Äî Enhanced Training Requirements for Concealed Carry Permits

The state tightens the requirements for concealed carry permits by introducing mandatory firearms training for applicants.

### 2021 ‚Äî Regulation of Untraceable Firearms

Arizona addresses concerns about untraceable ‚Äúghost guns‚Äù by introducing measures to regulate and track homemade firearms.

### 2023 ‚Äî ‚ÄúStand Your Ground‚Äù Law Implementation

Arizona introduces a ‚ÄúStand Your Ground‚Äù law, granting individuals the right to use deadly force in self-defense without a duty to retreat.
